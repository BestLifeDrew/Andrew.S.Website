Electronic & Structural Properties of Sc₂C MXenes

Co-authored peer-reviewed computational materials science research on Sc₂C MXenes — a class of two-dimensional transition metal carbides derived by selectively etching layers from MAX phase precursors. MXenes exhibit exceptional electrical conductivity, hydrophilicity, and tunable band structures, making them candidates for supercapacitors, electromagnetic interference shielding, and sensor applications.

Electronic band structures and density of states (DOS) were computed using Vienna Ab initio Simulation Package (VASP) under the DFT framework with Perdew-Burke-Ernzerhof (PBE) generalized gradient approximation. Atomic geometries and charge density distributions were visualized using VESTA. Findings were presented at the 2024 FSU Research Symposium before publication.
