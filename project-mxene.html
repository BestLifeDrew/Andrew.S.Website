Background

What are MXenes?

MXenes are a family of two-dimensional transition metal carbides, nitrides, and carbonitrides discovered in 2011. They are derived by selectively etching the A-element layers from layered MAX phase precursor materials (Mn+1AXn, where M is an early transition metal, A is a group IIIA or IVA element, and X is carbon or nitrogen), leaving behind atomically thin sheets.

Sc₂AlC → [HF etch] → Sc₂C + AlF₃

// MAX phase precursor etching to produce Sc₂C MXene

MXenes exhibit a rare combination of metallic electrical conductivity, hydrophilicity (water compatibility), mechanical strength, and a tunable electronic band structure. These properties make them candidates for supercapacitor electrodes, electromagnetic interference (EMI) shielding, 2D electronics, and sensor applications. Sc₂C (scandium carbide MXene) is a particularly interesting member of the family due to its lightweight scandium backbone and predicted electronic properties.

Research Objectives

What we set out to determine

The research focused on characterizing Sc₂C MXene computationally — without physical synthesis — using Density Functional Theory (DFT). The primary objectives were to determine the equilibrium crystal structure (lattice constants, bond lengths, atomic positions), compute the electronic band structure to classify metallic vs. semiconducting behavior, and analyze the density of states (DOS) to identify which atomic orbitals contribute to electronic properties near the Fermi level.

Methodology

DFT computational workflow

All calculations were performed using the Vienna Ab initio Simulation Package (VASP), an industry-standard DFT code. The exchange-correlation energy was approximated using the Perdew-Burke-Ernzerhof (PBE) generalized gradient approximation (GGA). Projector augmented-wave (PAW) pseudopotentials were used to describe electron-core interactions.

// STEP 01

Structure Setup

Built the Sc₂C unit cell in VESTA — defining lattice vectors, atomic coordinates, and the 2D periodic boundary conditions (vacuum layer added perpendicular to the sheet to prevent interlayer coupling in periodic DFT).

// STEP 02

Geometry Optimization

Ran ionic relaxation in VASP (IBRION=2, NSW=100) to allow atoms to move to their minimum-energy positions. Iterated until forces on all atoms were below 0.01 eV/Å and total energy changes per step were below 10⁻⁵ eV.

// STEP 03

Electronic Structure

Performed a non-self-consistent field (NSCF) calculation on a high-symmetry k-path through the Brillouin zone (Γ-M-K-Γ) to generate the electronic band structure plot, revealing band crossings at the Fermi level.

// STEP 04

Density of States

Computed the total and projected density of states (TDOS/PDOS) to identify orbital contributions from Sc d-orbitals and C p-orbitals to the electronic properties. Visualized charge density distributions in VESTA.

Key Findings

Results & significance

The DFT calculations revealed the equilibrium structural parameters of Sc₂C — including the optimized lattice constant and Sc–C bond lengths — consistent with prior theoretical predictions for MXene structures. The electronic band structure calculation showed bands crossing the Fermi level, indicating metallic character in the bare (unfunctionalized) Sc₂C MXene.

The density of states analysis revealed that electronic states near the Fermi level are dominated by Sc d-orbital contributions, with C p-orbitals contributing at lower binding energies. This orbital character has implications for the material's conductivity and its potential for surface functionalization chemistry — relevant to sensor and electrode applications.

Findings were first presented at the 2024 FSU Research Symposium, then revised and submitted for peer review, with the final paper published in the Journal of Nepal Physical Society in 2025.
